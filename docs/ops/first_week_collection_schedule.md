# First Week Collection Schedule

## Purpose
This schedule makes the first `7` days concrete enough to follow without turning collection into a project of its own.

The goal is a useful spread across common daily contexts, not perfect balance.

## Weekly Target
- default target: `3` rows per day
- stretch target: `4` rows on an active day
- quiet-day minimum: `1-2` rows if contexts are still varied

## 7-Day Plan

| Day | Main focus | Secondary focus | Daily target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | meal-related | owner-related | 3 |
| 2 | owner-related | door/window curiosity | 3 |
| 3 | play-related | one uncertain or noisy case if natural | 3 |
| 4 | noise/environment | owner-related | 3 |
| 5 | low-energy / bedtime | non-meal morning or evening clip | 3 |
| 6 | door/window curiosity | play or owner-related | 3 |
| 7 | fill gaps from the week | whichever context family is underrepresented | 3 |

## Contexts To Spread Across The Week
Try to cover these across the full week:

- meal-related
- owner-related
- play-related
- noise/environment-triggered
- low-energy / bedtime
- door/window curiosity

Do not try to force all of them every day. Spread them across the week.

## Good Enough Variety By Day 3
By day `3`, aim for:

- at least one meal-related clip
- at least one owner-related clip
- at least one play-related or curiosity-related clip
- at least `3-4` different primary labels total if the week allows it

If everything so far is meal-related, correct that on day 4.

## Good Enough Variety By Day 5
By day `5`, aim for:

- at least one environment-triggered clip if one occurs naturally
- at least one lower-energy or bedtime clip
- at least one `unknown` or noisy clip handled honestly if one occurs
- no single context family dominating most of the week

## Good Enough Variety By Day 7
By day `7`, aim for:

- all six context families represented at least once if naturally available
- enough spread that review scripts can separate context repetition from true disagreement
- enough non-meal logging that `food_like` is not carrying the whole week

## Simple Context Checkpoints

| Context family | Good Week 1 checkpoint |
| --- | --- |
| Meal-related | 2-4 rows |
| Owner-related | 2-4 rows |
| Play-related | 1-3 rows |
| Noise/environment | 1-2 rows |
| Low-energy / bedtime | 1-2 rows |
| Door/window curiosity | 1-2 rows |

These are guide rails, not quotas.

## Day-by-Day Notes

### Day 1
Start with easy wins. One meal-related row is fine, but do not let all three rows come from the same exact context.

### Day 2
Look for owner-related attention and a curiosity clip if possible.

### Day 3
Try to add a play-related example. If a messy clip happens naturally, log it honestly rather than hiding uncertainty.

### Day 4
Watch for sound-triggered or environment-triggered reactions. If none happen, use the day to add owner or curiosity variation instead.

### Day 5
Look for a low-energy or bedtime clip. These are easy to miss if you only log around meals.

### Day 6
Look for a door or window moment. If that is not available, choose whichever context family is still thin.

### Day 7
Use the final day to fill the biggest gap from the week rather than collecting more of the easiest pattern.

## Practical Rule
If the week starts drifting toward meal-only clips:

- do not force fewer logs
- just make the next capture choice more deliberate

That is enough for Week 1.
